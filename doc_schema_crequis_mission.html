A TEI Customization for Crequi's Mission Project

This TEI ODD defines a TEI customization for encoding letters from the French Archives Nationales related to CrÃ©qui-Montfort's scientific mission during 1904 in South America. The project aims to create a comprehensive database that documents the mission in detail, shedding light on the roles and interactions of various actors, including institutions, government ministries, members of the mission, scientists, and museums. This database will enable a better understanding of the missionâs progression, from its initial planning to the dispersal and exhibition of the collected objects. Additionally, the project seeks to enhance the description of the archival collections from which the letters are extracted. The letters encoded in this project are drawn from two specific archival sources:

  • The mission's file, which contains administrative and logistical documents related to the organization and execution of the scientific mission.
  • The exhibition dossier, which documents the display of the objects collected during the mission at the MusÃ©e du TrocadÃ©ro.

To achieve its goals, the TEI customization incorporates a structured <teiHeader> designed to facilitate in-depth documentation. Key components include:

  • A <sourceDesc> section containing <listPerson>, <listOrg>, and <listPlace>, which provide an organized representation of individuals, organizations, and places referenced in the letters.
  • A <profileDesc> section that includes a <correspDesc> element to document the events of sending and receiving correspondence, offering insights into the communication flow and its historical context.

The body of the letters is less constrained, allowing for flexibility in encoding the main text, but it requires the inclusion of essential structural components such as an <opener> and a <closer>. This ensures consistency while maintaining the authenticity of the original documents. Overall, this TEI customization serves as a foundational tool to explore and analyze the dynamics of this significant scientific mission. By creating a database and enriching archival descriptions, it contributes to a deeper understanding of the mission's historical, scientific, and cultural significance.
